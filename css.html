Computer Science Society

Department of Computer Science, Aligarh Muslim University

Introduction

The Computer Science Society- CSS (Formerly the Area of Dominant Coders Club - ADC) is an initiative of the students of the Department of Computer Science, Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh. It's a platform to share the knowledge of Computer Science among all the interested members of the Faculty of Science.

Inspired by the vision of Sir Syed to create a scientific society, the ADC club was formed in December 2018 and has been consistently working since then. It consists of a team of B.Sc. (Hons.) and MCA Students of the University having technical insight and a passion for programming. Here we mentor, guide, share, and learn from each other regarding the latest technology giving students the much-needed exposure for industrial demands and global trends.

A Walk Through The History

December 2018: The Inception

The Area of Dominant Coders Club (ADC) came into existence with 27 registered members from B.Sc. (Hons.) Computer Applications I and II Year. The initial coordinators were Mr. Arish Rehman, Mr. Mohammad Areeb, Mr. Mohammad Umair, and Mr. Jaanbaaz Akhtar.

September 2019: The Revision

Under Miss. Noor Fatima (Coordinator) and Mr. Syed Mohib Raza (Secretary), the club expanded to include both B.Sc. and MCA students, growing to over 129 registered members. Partnerships were formed with Zerynth and Microsoft, leading to the organization of AMUHacks 1.0.

March 2021: The Recognition

The club received official recognition and financial support from the Hon'able Vice-Chancellor, Aligarh Muslim University, and was renamed as the Computer Science Society.

Objectives

  • To bring about innovations in the technological sphere
  • To learn beyond the classroom level
  • To provide consultancy to the industries/institutes/universities
  • To instill computer-related technical knowledge in the students
  • To help students realize their interests at an early level
  • To make them aware of the latest trends in Computer Science
  • To organize technical events and coding competitions
  • To invite experts to deliver talks on major technologies
  • To conduct sessions to improve Soft-Skills
  • To explore areas to generate revenue

Outcomes

Career Opportunities

Better job opportunities and improved performance in recruitment drives

Technical Skills

Development of applications, websites, ML models, and IoT products

Soft Skills

Better communication skills and confidence in representing the University

Professional Growth

Internship opportunities and CV enhancement

How The Society Operates

  • Committee selection by the President at the beginning of each academic year
  • Academic calendar design with planned events and tentative dates
  • Annual orientation event for newly admitted students
  • Interview-based selection for new student mentors
  • Event planning in consultation with teacher mentors
  • Systematic distribution of responsibilities for event organization
  • Final decision-making authority rests with the President
